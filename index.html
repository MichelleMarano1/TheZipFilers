Problem Background

Shorelines are one of the most essential ecological features that determine the overall health of a water body's ecosystem, therefore maintaining these shorelines are key to managing water bodies in a sustainable manner. This team has been assigned to create a visual and interactive display of these land use changes around the shoreline of Lake Scugog in Ontario, Canada from aerial imagery over a span of more than 65 years.

Introduction

Agriculture and cottage country have been engulfing our lakes and shorelines, putting pressure on the living ecosystems. A full analysis is therefore necessary so that these effects can be seen.​

Process

A land cover classification called an ecological land classification, or ELC is used to determine changes to land cover and to determine the composition of a landscape. In the ELC's, land types include: Meadow, Agriculture, Forest, Aggregate Pits, Open Spaces, Open Water, Rural Development, Urban Development, and Wetlands. Through these 9 classifications in the ELCs, we can track the change of land use between years.

Outcome

Indicating how the land cover around Lake Scugog has occurred allows us to monitor these areas and determine which areas are most at risk of impacting the lake. Understanding where these at-risk areas are can help us manage lakes in a sustainable manner and provide people an explanation of how development affects shorelines.

Our Project

Timeline of our Methodologies

  • March 21st - May 16th

    Data Acquisition

    We started the project with Pre-processing beginning with the acquisition of the aerial imagery, which Kawartha Conservation provided, the dataset included the years 1988, 2002, and 2018. As group we collected 1954 which we obtain from the University of Toronto Map and Data Library.

  • May 14th - May 31st

    Aerial Interpretation

    We brought our imagery into ArcGIS Pro and began georeferencing using the spline method. We used many control points to maintain accuracy with a very small RMS error, which was less than 1.0. After this process was completed, we where then ready to classify both 1954 and 1988 within PCI Geomatica. We used the object analysts’ segmentations to do this, after the objects were created, we used testing sites to create 9 different classes and manually classified each segment.

  • May 31st - June 8th

    Spatial Analysis

    We created a model that allows you to input two different layers which then adds fields, then using the dissolve tool it aggregates specific features based on the gridcode field which corresponds to the land use type field. We then created a python code that calculated a newly created field called landuse_class, and this code saves additional steps throughout this process. After this process was done, we used the intersect tool that takes the data from both Landuse_class field and combines it into one field. The result of this tool only shows what sections between the years were overlapping.

  • June 16th - June 25th

    Final Product

    Our final StoryMap includes all 8 of the content seen on screen, we created a user-friendly web application that allows us to display our classifications and change detection analysis, that is easy and digestible for anyone who wants to know more about Lake Scugog’s ever changing shoreline. With the help of images, animations and configurable apps the storymap takes the user though our analysis.
